Braised Beef Recipe

Description

Braising is a cooking method that involves slowly cooking meat in a covered pot with some sort of liquid. In the case of this recipe, chuck roast is braised in a mixture of beef broth and red wine. Braising is similar to stewing, but it requires less liquid.

Ingridients

  • Boneless beef chuck roast
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Cremini mushrooms
  • red wine
  • Beef broth,

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients and preheat
  2. Season beef pieces evenly on all sides
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat.
  4. Return skillet to medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons butter to the drippings in the pan. When butter has melted add onion, mushrooms and carrots and cook 5 minutes, stirring often, Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  5. Stir in wine and scrape to release any more bits from the pan.
  6. Pour in beef broth just enough that it comes up 2/3 of the way up on the beef
  7. Cover and place in preheated oven until meat is very tender, 2 to 2 1/2 hours.
  8. Serve and enjoy!
